Japanese Milk Bread



This Japanese Milk Bread is made using a water roux or tangzhong, which is a heated water and flour roux
that is added to the bread dough. This creates an incredibly soft and fluffy bread that stays moist for days.


Ingredients

Makes 1 loaf

Water Roux

  • 1/3 cup bread flour
  • 1 cup water

Bread Dough

  • 2 1/2 cups bread flour
  • 3tbsp + 2tsp sugar
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 120g water roux (about 1/2 of the mixture you made)
  • 2 tsp instant yeast
  • 3 tbsp butter (room temperature, cut into small pieces)

Directions

  1. Combine the flour, salt, sugar and instant yeast in a bowl.

  2. Make a well in the center. Add in all wet ingredients: milk, egg and water roux mixture.
  3. Begin mixing the flour mixture with a spoon until your dough comes together.

  4. Place the dough on to a clean countertop. Continue kneading with your hands until the dough is smooth, not too sticky on the surface and elastic.

  5. Knead the dough into a ball shape. Take a large bowl and grease with oil. Place dough into greased bowl and cover with a wet towel. Let it proof until it's doubled in size, about 40 minutes.

  6. Transfer to a clean surface. Divide the dough into four equal portions. Knead into balls. Cover with cling wrap, let rest for 15 minutes.

  7. Flatten each peice of dough into a oval with a rolling pin. Fold the edges of the oval inwards, in thirds.

  8. Flip dough over with the folds facing down, and flatten dough with rolling pin.

  9. Flip dough over so the folds face up. Now roll the dough up.

  10. Place the 4 rolls seam side down into a lightly greased 9 x 5 inch loaf pan. Cover them with a cloth or plastic wrap and let them rise until double the size, about 40 minutes.

  11. Beat an egg and brush egg mixture on top to create shiny eggwash finish.

  12. Bake at 325 degrees F for 30-40 minutes.
